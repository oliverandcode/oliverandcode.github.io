After three years of telling everyone that VS Code was good enough, I finally made the jump to Neovim. Six months later, I have opinions.

## The inciting incident

It started with a slow extension. My TypeScript language server was eating 800MB of RAM and the editor was visibly lagging on a medium-sized monorepo. I'd been curious about Neovim for years, and this felt like the moment.

## What I gained

The things I actually got from the switch:

- **Speed** — genuinely instant startup, snappy file navigation
- **Modal editing** — I thought I'd hate it; I was wrong
- **Composability** — tools do one thing well and you wire them together
- **Full control** — every behavior is configurable, nothing is magic

## What I miss

Being honest: VS Code has better out-of-the-box support for almost everything. Debugging, git blame inline, remote containers. Setting those up in Neovim is a project.

## The verdict

Six months in, I'm not going back. But I wouldn't recommend the switch to someone early in their career — there are higher-leverage things to learn first.
